Course Outcomes

  • To explore the historical, cultural, theatrical, and literary facets of Shakespeare's plays and sonnets;
  • To perform short scenes from Shakespeare's comedies and tragedies;
  • To conduct research on the performance history of one Shakespearean play;
  • To identify themes and formal elements of early modern English drama and poetry;
  • To analyze literature through an interdisciplinary lens; and
  • To compose an argumentative research paper

Required Materials

  • The Norton Shakespeare, E-book (ISBN: 978-1-324-07179-2)

  • A computer or laptop with Internet access

External Resources

  • Open Source Shakespeare
    • This free resource provides full text of all of Shakespeare's plays, as well as important features such as a concordance and the ability to search for speeches by character.
  • The Globe
    • Shakespeare's most well-known playhouse. This site offers information on the history of the theatre and how research is still continued today.
  • YouTube
    • This might seem like an odd resource, but you can find many film clips and full dramatic performances of Shakespeare's plays posted to YouTube.
  • LibriVox
    • This site offers free recordings of most of Shakespeare's works. Feel free to listen along as you read, as some of the difficult language and scenarios become clearer when we hear how the words are spoken.
